| [ADR-0290](0290-vmaf-tune-nvenc-adapters.md) | NVIDIA NVENC codec adapters for `vmaf-tune` (`h264_nvenc`, `hevc_nvenc`, `av1_nvenc`). Ships one adapter per output codec under `tools/vmaf-tune/src/vmaftune/codec_adapters/` plus a shared `_nvenc_common.py` helper that owns the mnemonic → `p1`..`p7` preset map and the `[0, 51]` constant-quantizer window. Mnemonic preset names (`ultrafast`..`placebo`) are reused verbatim from the libx264 adapter so cross-codec sweeps stay semantically aligned. Hardware encoders are 10–100× faster than software at the cost of ~3–5 VMAF points at matched bitrate; the R-D curve is codec-distinct, not codec-shifted, which is why NVENC ships as separate codec entries rather than a flag on `libx264`. Codec one-hot expansion in `fr_regressor_v2` (currently 6-slot) is a separate follow-up.
